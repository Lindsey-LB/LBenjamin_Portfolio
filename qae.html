Projects


Supplier AQL Implementation Shopware Transition
2020 2019
This project's aim was to formalise Acceptable Quality Levels for all procured finished products and packaging materials by liaising with Product Development & Production teams to understand what was required of the product, and understand the limitations of the processing equipment.
The defect descriptions, their allowable defect percentage, the inspection methods and the processes for rejecting non-conforming materials and products were captured in a controlled document signed by both parties. Thereafter, the agreed and documented processes were mirrored on SAP to implement the change.
 This project's aim was to transition from physical check sheets for production QC to digital records that are immediately available for management and quality teams to review.
My role was not only to develop inspections on Shopware, but to advise the developer on the requirements of the system from a process and compliance perspective, and to test and feed back on any bugs or deficiencies in the system.
Once developed, the system needed to be integrated into the current QMS, to ensure that compliance was maintained. This included updating all SOPs and controlled documents, retraining and assessment, and developing a contingency process in the case that the system went offline.
Digital Production Resource Tool Transition Operator Control
2017-2020 2016
The aim of this project was to move from a production resource tool which consisted of a physical sample, a bill of materials printed from SAP and basic text instructions to a digital production resource tool that contained everything needed to assess the quality of the product.
The digital PRTs were grouped by their product groups and included bills of materials, technical drawings, purchase order texts for each material and approved artwork. A visual process flow replaced the basic text instructionsan and the controlled documents relevant to the products were listed within the document.
This project reduced the time waste of retrieving, validating and issuing physical PRTs as well as the movement waste of production having to liaise with QC and Packaging should their be additional data needed that was not included in the physical PRT. 		The aim of this project was to create a system where the production staff owned responsibility for the quality conformance of the products they produced. At the time of implementation, the production QC team performed hourly inspections for conformance and reported back to the production team if any defects were found. This change created a sense of ownership on the part of production, as well as the ability to continuously monitor compliance and quickly rectify any problems, which reduced waste.
The QC production role was changed to a QA Analyst role, focussed on supporting production through training interventions and more focus on Supplier relationships to improve the quality of the packaging materials.
The QA Analysts also monitored and improved the system through regular auditing, root-cause analysis and corrective actions.
