Profit Improvement Projects

Aerosol Performance Optimisation Downtime Reduction
2014 2013
This profit improvement project was projected to produce approximately R604 500 in cost savings, by reducing the amount of downtime, reducing the amount of short stoppages and increasing the OEE of the Aerosol production line. Targets for OEE, Mean Time Between Stops and downtime were set, and tracked through weekly management meetings. Causes for all stops (short stops and major downtime) were recorded per machine and during management meetings these causes were analysed. Corrective and preventative actions were implemented to prevent recurrence of these causes.Ultimately, the project yielded R288 000 in savings (48% of estimate) mainly due to major machine breakdowns which required parts with long lead times to resolve. However, the learnings achieved through the duration of the project enabled the production teams to consistently achieve their productivity targets for many years to come.
 This profit improvement project was projected to produce approximately R525 000 in cost savings, by reducing the amount of downtime experienced by the Aerosol production line. During the problem definition stage, it was found that the majority of downtime was caused by the packing processes. Through analyses of downtime causes, it was found that packaging material quality, and machine setup caused most of the downtime incurred. Training and assessment of production staff was done once machine setup was standardised. Material specifications for secondary packaging were further defined to improve the performance of the material in the machines and trials were done on improved materials. Ultimately, the project yielded R346 000 in savings (66% of estimate). Some improvements(such as trials) came at the cost of savings but key learnings were utilised to improve standard operating procedures, which meant that further cost savings were accumulated beyond the finalisation of the project.
